He is serving a 25-year sentence for corruption, among other charges. </p> <p> Keiko Fujimori, 41, originally seemed headed to easy victory: she won the first round of voting by a 20 percentage-point margin, but by Sunday, her wide lead over Kuczynski had disappeared. </p> <p> In a quick count of 84.5% of sample ballots, pollster Ipsos-Apoyo said Kuczynski won 50.9% of votes, with Fujimori winning 49.1%, Reuters reported. </p> <p> Another local pollster, Gfk, which was the most accurate in predicting results from the first round, showed Kuczynski winning by more than two percentage points, while a third poll by CPI showed Fujimori winning by three points. </p> <figure> <img src="..&#x2F;..&#x2F;images&#x2F;sample&#x2F;landscape-4x3-mountains.jpg" alt="Mountains" /> <figcaption> Lorem ipsum dolor sit amet, consectetur adipisicing elit. Meanwhile, Kuczynski&#39;s campaign aides filed into his home to await official results, flashing &quot;V&#39; for victory&quot; signs. </p> <p> A potential swing vote in a close race could be the 885,000 Peruvians eligible to vote abroad — about 3.8% of the electorate, AP reported. </p> <p> Kuczynski, known widely as &quot;PPK,&quot; supported Fujimori in a 2011 presidential runoff, won by outgoing President Ollanta Humala. But he has campaigned aggressively against his younger opponent, telling supporters at a rally last week in Lima, &quot;Peru is on the threshold of becoming a narco-state.&quot; </p> <p> The reference wasn’t just to the elder Fujimori’s well-known ties to corruption, organized crime and death squads, but an attempt to draw attention to a string of scandals that have hobbled the younger Fujimori in the final stretch — most notably a report that one of her big fundraisers and the secretary general of her party is the target of a U.S. Drug Enforcement Administration investigation, AP reported. </p> <p> Peru is the world’s largest producer of cocaine. </p> <p> Kuczynski benefited from a last-minute endorsement by the third-place finisher in the first round of voting, leftist congresswoman
